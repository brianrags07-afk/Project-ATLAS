
# PROJECT ATLAS
# Discovery Specification
Version: 1.0.0

---

## Mission

ATLAS exists to discover, validate, and continuously refine baseball knowledge.

ATLAS does not assume.

ATLAS discovers.

---

## Scientific Method

Every discovery follows the same lifecycle.

1. Observe
2. Measure
3. Collect Evidence
4. Discover Interactions
5. Estimate Effect Size
6. Measure Sample Size
7. Measure Stability
8. Validate Out-of-Sample
9. Assign Confidence
10. Promote to Knowledge
11. Monitor Continuously
12. Increase, Reduce, or Retire Knowledge

---

## Observation

Everything measurable before first pitch may become evidence.

Examples

Starting Pitcher

Bullpen

Weather

Park

Travel

Rest

Series

Pitch Types

Velocity

Movement

Lineups

Manager

Umpire

Market (future)

No observation is assumed important.

---

## Baseball Questions

ATLAS learns by answering questions.

Examples

WHEN does this team score?

WHY?

AGAINST WHO?

UNDER WHAT CONDITIONS?

BY HOW MUCH?

HOW CONFIDENT?

Every engine should answer questions rather than calculate statistics.

---

## Evidence

Evidence consists of observed historical outcomes.

Evidence never contains conclusions.

Evidence only measures relationships.

---

## Interactions

Interactions occur whenever two or more baseball variables combine.

Examples

Ground Ball Pitcher

×

Fly Ball Offense

Velocity

×

Patient Offense

Wind Out

×

Power Team

Elite Bullpen

×

One Run Lead

Pitch Mix

×

Pitch Type Run Values

Interactions are measured.

Never assumed.

---

## Entity Specific Learning

Interactions are never universal.

Every interaction is evaluated independently for

Teams

Pitchers

Batters

Bullpens

Managers

Lineups

Parks

Umpires

---

## Target Specific Learning

Knowledge must remain independent for each prediction target.

Moneyline

Runline

Totals

Over

Under

YRFI

NRFI

First Five

Pitcher Props

Batter Props

An interaction may matter for one target and have no meaningful effect on another.

---

## Knowledge Promotion

An interaction becomes knowledge only after sufficient evidence.

Knowledge requires

Effect Size

Sample Size

Confidence

Stability

Validation

Knowledge may later be downgraded or retired.

---

## Continuous Learning

Every completed game becomes new evidence.

New evidence may

Increase confidence

Reduce confidence

Strengthen knowledge

Weaken knowledge

Retire knowledge

No discovery is permanent.

---

## Bias Control

ATLAS begins with zero baseball opinions.

Every relationship must earn importance through evidence.

No interaction is guaranteed to matter.

No statistic is guaranteed to predict.

"No meaningful effect" is a successful scientific result.

---

## Explainability

Every prediction must be explainable.

ATLAS should identify

Which evidence contributed.

Which interactions mattered.

How much each interaction changed probability.

How confident ATLAS is in every conclusion.

---

## Final Principle

ATLAS is not a prediction engine.

ATLAS is a scientific discovery engine that continuously learns baseball.

Predictions are a consequence of understanding.
